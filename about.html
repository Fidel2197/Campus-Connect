About

Your friendly User Interactive student-built platform for tutoring, events, and networking.

Campus Connect is a student-built platform designed to make campus life feel smaller, friendlier, and easier to navigate. It brings tutoring, events, groups, messaging, and skill-sharing into one place so students can find what they need without digging through endless group chats or flyers.

At the core of Campus Connect are student profiles. Each profile highlights a student’s major, year, skills, interests, availability, and what they’re looking for—whether that’s a tutor, a study partner, new friends, or networking opportunities. By organizing this information in a simple, structured way, the platform makes it easier to connect with the right people at the right time.

Academic support is a big focus. Campus Connect includes a tutoring and request hub where students can post offers (subjects, rates, mode, availability) or create requests (course, level, preferred times, budget). The system is designed so students can quickly browse, filter, and match with options that fit their schedule and needs.

The platform also supports recurring groups—like study circles, project teams, or clubs— with simple group spaces for shared info, member details, and links to events. Built-in messaging lets students contact tutors, event hosts, or group members directly without needing phone numbers or separate apps, and an interactive campus map ties everything back to real locations such as study spots and meeting spaces.

Campus Connect even extends into a skill-exchange marketplace where students can swap languages, music, creative skills, fitness help, and more. The overall goal is to support both academic success and personal growth by making it easier for every student to find support, opportunities, and community.

Why We Built It

Campus Connect started from a simple question: why is it so hard to find the right people and resources when they’re already on the same campus? We saw classmates struggling to locate tutors, events with open seats that students never heard about, and groups that wanted more members but didn’t have a central place to reach them.

This project was created to address that gap by combining profiles, tutoring, events, groups, messaging, and maps into a single experience built from a student perspective. It is currently a GUI-only prototype built to satisfy the Campus Connect course rubric: backend features like password hashing, email delivery, and push notifications are represented through UI placeholders, alerts, and localStorage simulations rather than a live production server.

Even as a prototype, the design aims to show how a real version of Campus Connect could make it easier for students to succeed academically, meet people they might not otherwise cross paths with, and feel more at home on campus.
